About

Community Cultivators

Our vision is to create community through gardening for people. As horticultural therapists, we are trained to work with people with diverse challenges, and combined with best gardening practices we are able to create successful experiences for all gardeners.

Community Cultivators brings people together through organic gardening by facilitating relationships and creating opportunities for successful activity. We work with organizations such as assisted living and other residential sites to develop programming for existing gardens. Our staff can provide weekly garden sessions or monthly workshops. We can assist in the planning of garden events and activities, and we can train staff and caregivers to enable others to work safely in the garden.

Community Cultivators can assist in the design of accessible gardens, whether in a backyard or on a porch, or in a residential community. Developing appropriate educational programming to support the gardens and gardeners ensures a successful experience. Whether teaching people how to grow their own organic food, or enabling a person to revel in the joy of growing and tending flowers, our passion is in creating access to gardening.

Kate Bodin

Kate Bodin, MEd, Registered Horticultural Therapist, is the owner of Community Cultivators LLC. She completed the Legacy Health/Portland Community College Horticultural Therapy Program, and holds a Masters of Education in Creative Arts and Learning and Activity Director Certification from the State of Oregon.

Previously, Kate oversaw the horticultural therapy program at Earthtones NW in Portland, OR and has implemented several thriving horticultural therapy programs for older adults throughout the region. She developed and implemented a comprehensive horticultural therapy internship program at Earthtones, and continues to train interns through her work with Community Cultivators. Kate is a part-time faculty member at Portland Community College, where she teaches horticulture. Kate’s experience includes working with people diagnosed with dementia, physical, behavioral and cognitive disabilities, as well as older adults in assisted living and wellness settings. She has worked with people residing in private homes, assisted living and long-term care sites, as well as those in hospitals and day programs. Kate is an avid and experienced gardener, educator and outdoorswoman. A passionate cook, she loves creating nourishing meals from the produce she grows in her garden.
